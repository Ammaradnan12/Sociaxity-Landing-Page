Social anxiety disorder (SAD) is an anxiety disorder where people fear being judged by others and avoid social situations. It goes beyond normal nerves and can be highly distressing and impairing.

Factors Social Anxiety

  • Genetic factors
  • Overprotective parenting
  • Limited social experiences
  • Memories of public humiliation
  • History of bullying or public humiliation

Recognise Social Anxiety

  • Fear of criticism.
  • Physical symptoms.
  • Avoiding social events.
  • Fear of embarrassment.
  • Occasional panic attacks.
  • Difficulty when observed.
  • Worrying about everyday activities.

Overcome Social Anxiety

  • Reflect on your thoughts and behaviors in social situations like keeping a diary can help.
  • Practice relaxation techniques like stress-relieving breathing exercises.
  • Divide daunting situations into smaller steps to ease anxiety gradually.
  • Concentrate on what people are saying, instead of assuming the worst.
